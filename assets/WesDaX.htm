Welcome to WesDaX, Westat's Data Explorer! Designed for quick and easy access to valuable insights, WesDaX - through its dynamic, online system - leverages and organizes complex data into illuminating visualizations and actionable tables.

Easy to deploy and navigate, WesDaX offers users an efficient and effective tool that expands the utility of research. Join numerous other projects in getting the most out of your data today!

Features
Table Builder quickly and easily generates statistics (frequencies, percentiles, means), tables and figures that can be cited in studies, presentations, reports, or briefings
Weighted and unweighted data can be displayed in frequency distributions, cross-tabs, means, percentiles, confidence intervals, and statistical testing
Custom reports written in SAS® can be parameterized to enable users to optimize/tailor their output with seamless integration. These results can be displayed in various formats from Excel to PDF.
Robust confidentiality treatments are available — even though case-level data are not displayed, disclosure risks may remain if non-public data are used
WesVar® is the analytic engine for WesDaX and analyzes data from
simple to the most complex sample designs. Learn more ›
Out of the box design allows for quick set up with minimal
labor for projects and datasets of any size

Research Data Sets

Utilize public use data sets to gain valuable insights into the results of large national surveys and census. Analyze huge amounts of data to discover trends and other useful visualizations. Select any of the research data sets below to begin exploring.

The American Community Survey (ACS) is the largest survey in the US and covers topics related to disability, educational attainment, employment, journey to work, income, language proficiency, migration, and housing characteristics.
The Current Population Survey (CPS) Annual Social and Economic Supplement (ASEC) is a large national survey that provides data generally relating to
income, poverty, and health insurance coverage.
The Common Core of Data (CCD) provides comprehensive data (e.g., enrollment by grade) on a national census of public elementary and secondary schools and school districts.
